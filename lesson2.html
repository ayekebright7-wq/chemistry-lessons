Lesson 2

"Like Dissolves Like" - The Polarity Rule

Understanding why oil and water don't mix

20 minutes Beginner Level WASSCE Topic: Molecular Interactions

Teacher's Explanation

"Remember when your mother washes grease from pots? She uses soap, not just water. Why? Because grease and water don't 'like' each other! This lesson explains why some substances mix while others don't."

Ghanaian Kitchen Example

Making Groundnut Soup

When you make groundnut soup with palm oil, you notice the oil floats on top of the water-based soup. No matter how much you stir, they eventually separate. This is because palm oil is non-polar while water is polar - they don't "like" each other!

Key Concepts: Polarity

Polar Molecules

Have positive (δ+) and negative (δ-) ends due to uneven electron sharing.

Examples:
  • Water (H₂O)
  • Ethanol (C₂H₅OH)
  • Table salt (NaCl)

Non-polar Molecules

No charged ends - electrons are shared equally.

Examples:
  • Palm oil
  • Petrol
  • Kerosene
  • Carbon tetrachloride

The Golden Rule

"Like dissolves like" - polar dissolves polar, non-polar dissolves non-polar.

Why it matters:
  • Water (polar) + Salt (polar) = ✓
  • Oil (non-polar) + Water (polar) = ✗
  • Oil (non-polar) + Petrol (non-polar) = ✓

Water: The Polar Molecule

Oxygen attracts electrons more strongly than hydrogen, creating partial negative charge (δ⁻) on oxygen and partial positive charges (δ⁺) on hydrogens.

This polarity allows water to dissolve other polar substances like salt and sugar.

Polar Substances (Dissolve in Water)

  • ✓ Salt (NaCl)
  • ✓ Sugar (C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁)
  • ✓ Vinegar (CH₃COOH)

Non-polar Substances (Don't dissolve in Water)

  • ✗ Palm oil
  • ✗ Wax
  • ✗ Grease

Lesson Recap

Key Points to Remember:

  • Polar molecules have charged ends (like water)
  • Non-polar molecules have no charged ends (like oil)
  • The rule: Polar dissolves polar, non-polar dissolves non-polar
  • Water is the universal solvent for polar substances

WASSCE Tip

Always mention "polarity" when explaining why substances dissolve or don't dissolve. For example: "Oil doesn't dissolve in water because oil is non-polar while water is polar, and like dissolves like."
