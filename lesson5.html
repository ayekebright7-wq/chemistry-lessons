Lesson 5

Solubility Rules & Predicting Precipitation

Master the rules to predict when solids will form in solutions

30 minutes Advanced Level WASSCE Topic: Precipitation Reactions

Teacher's Explanation

"When two solutions mix, sometimes they stay clear, sometimes a solid forms (precipitate). Chemists have rules to predict this - crucial for WASSCE exams! Learn these rules and you'll be able to predict chemical reactions like a pro."

Lab Demonstration

The "Golden Rain" Experiment

In chemistry labs, when lead nitrate solution mixes with potassium iodide solution, a beautiful yellow precipitate of lead iodide forms instantly. This is a classic precipitation reaction that follows the solubility rules you'll learn today.

Essential Solubility Rules

Always Soluble (Memorize These!)

  • All Group 1 (Alkali metal) compounds: Li⁺, Na⁺, K⁺, etc.
  • All ammonium (NH₄⁺) compounds
  • All nitrates (NO₃⁻) - e.g., KNO₃, NaNO₃
  • All acetates (CH₃COO⁻)
  • Most chlorides (Cl⁻) - except AgCl, PbCl₂, Hg₂Cl₂

Generally Insoluble (Exceptions Exist)

  • Most carbonates (CO₃²⁻): Except Group 1 and NH₄⁺
  • Most phosphates (PO₄³⁻): Except Group 1 and NH₄⁺
  • Most sulfides (S²⁻): Except Group 1, 2, and NH₄⁺
  • Most hydroxides (OH⁻): Except Group 1, Ba²⁺, Sr²⁺, Ca²⁺

Important Exceptions to Remember

  • AgCl, PbCl₂: Insoluble chlorides
  • BaSO₄, PbSO₄, CaSO₄: Insoluble sulfates
  • AgBr, AgI: Insoluble silver halides

Memory Tip: "PMS" - Pb²⁺, Ag⁺, Hg²⁺ form insoluble chlorides. Also remember "CASH" - Ca²⁺, Ag⁺, Sr²⁺, Ba²⁺, Pb²⁺ sulfates are mostly insoluble.

Ionic Equation for the Reaction

Full equation: AgNO₃(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO₃(aq)

Ionic equation: Ag⁺(aq) + NO₃⁻(aq) + Na⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq) → AgCl(s) + Na⁺(aq) + NO₃⁻(aq)

Net ionic equation: Ag⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq) → AgCl(s)

The net ionic equation shows only the ions that actually react to form the precipitate. Spectator ions (Na⁺ and NO₃⁻) are cancelled out.

Lesson Recap

Key Points to Remember:

  • Group 1 and NH₄⁺ compounds: Always soluble
  • Nitrates and acetates: Always soluble
  • Most chlorides soluble: Except Ag⁺, Pb²⁺, Hg₂²⁺
  • Most sulfates soluble: Except Ba²⁺, Pb²⁺, Ca²⁺
  • Precipitation occurs when insoluble product forms

WASSCE Tip

In practical exams, if you see a white precipitate when mixing solutions, think: AgCl, BaSO₄, or CaCO₃. Yellow precipitate = PbI₂ or AgI. Memorize the colors of common precipitates!
