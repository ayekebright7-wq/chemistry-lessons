Lesson 1

What is Solubility?

Understanding the basic concepts with everyday examples from Ghana

15 minutes Beginner Level WASSCE Topic: Solutions

Teacher's Explanation

"Think about making your morning Milo. When you stir the powder into hot water, it disappears, right? That's solubility in action! But if you try to stir sand into your Milo, it just sits at the bottom. Solubility explains why some things 'disappear' in liquid while others don't."

Real Life Example

Making Sugar Water for Sobolo

When you make Ghana's favorite hibiscus drink, you dissolve sugar in water. The sugar (solute) disappears into the water (solvent) to make sweet water (solution). This wouldn't work with sand - it would just sink to the bottom!

Key Definitions You Must Know

Solute

The substance that gets dissolved.

Examples:
  • Sugar in tea
  • Salt in stew
  • Milo powder in hot water

Solvent

The substance that does the dissolving.

Examples:
  • Water (universal solvent)
  • Palm oil in cooking
  • Petrol in cleaning grease

Solution

Homogeneous mixture of solute and solvent.

Examples:
  • Sweet tea
  • Salt water
  • Medicinal syrup

Remember: A solution is always clear (transparent) and the solute particles are too small to see - they don't settle or scatter light!

Lesson Recap

Key Points to Remember:

  • Solubility = how well something dissolves
  • Three players: solute, solvent, solution
  • Solutions are always clear and homogeneous
  • Solubility depends on the nature of substances

WASSCE Tip

In exams, always identify the solute and solvent correctly. Remember: The substance present in smaller amount is usually the solute, but the solvent is what does the dissolving!
